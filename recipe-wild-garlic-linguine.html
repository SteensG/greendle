Step by Step

Method

01
01
Toast the hazelnuts
5 min

Place the hazelnuts in a dry frying pan over medium heat. Toast, tossing frequently, for 4–5 minutes until golden and fragrant. Tip onto a board, allow to cool slightly, then roughly crush with the flat of a knife. Set aside.

02
02
Blanch the nettles
3 min

Fill a large pot with well-salted water and bring to a rolling boil. Drop the nettle tips in for exactly 30 seconds — this neutralises the sting. Remove with a slotted spoon into iced water. Squeeze out excess water, roughly chop. Keep the boiling water for your pasta.

03
03
Wilt the wild garlic
4 min

Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a wide sauté pan over medium-low heat. Add the shallots and a pinch of salt. Cook gently for 3–4 minutes until softened but not coloured. Add the minced garlic for 1 minute. Add the wild garlic leaves and toss until just wilted — 60 to 90 seconds. Remove from heat.

04
04
Cook the linguine
9–11 min

Add the linguine to the boiling water. Cook according to packet instructions minus 1 minute — it will finish in the pan. Reserve 150ml of pasta water before draining. This starchy liquid is your secret sauce.

05
05
Bring it together
4 min

Return the wild garlic pan to low heat. Add the drained linguine, chopped nettles, remaining olive oil, and a splash of pasta water. Toss vigorously for 2 minutes, adding more pasta water as needed for a silky, emulsified sauce. Add lemon zest, a squeeze of lemon juice, and half the Parmesan. Toss again.

06
06
Plate and finish
Serve now

Divide between warmed bowls. Top with crushed hazelnuts, remaining Parmesan, a crack of black pepper, flat-leaf parsley, and a final drizzle of olive oil. Serve immediately — this dish does not wait.

Chef's Notes
A few things that make the difference
💧
Pasta water is your sauce

Don't skip reserving it. The starchy water is what makes the sauce coat the pasta rather than pool at the bottom.

📅
Wild garlic season is short

Peak harvest is mid-March to late April. Outside this window, substitute with 2 cloves garlic and a handful of baby spinach.

🧤
Nettles without fear

Use tongs or rubber gloves to handle raw nettles. Once blanched for 30 seconds they are completely safe and taste wonderfully earthy.
